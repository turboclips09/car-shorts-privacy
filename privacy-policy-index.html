Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

This privacy policy describes how the Car Shorts Bot ("the App") collects, uses, and handles information when interacting with the YouTube Data API.

Who operates this app

This is a personal automation tool operated by an individual creator to manage their own YouTube channel. It is not a public-facing service and is not distributed to other users.

What data we access

The App connects to a single YouTube channel owned and authorized by the operator. Through Google's OAuth 2.0 system, it accesses:

  • YouTube video upload — to upload video content created by the operator
  • YouTube read-only data — to read view counts, likes, and comment counts on the operator's own uploaded videos for performance tracking
  • YouTube full access (force-ssl) — to set video thumbnails and post comments on the operator's own videos

What we do not do

  • We do not collect, store, or share any data belonging to third parties or viewers
  • We do not access any YouTube account other than the one explicitly authorized by the operator
  • We do not sell, rent, or share any data with third parties
  • We do not use accessed data for advertising purposes

Data storage

Video metadata (titles, descriptions, view counts) generated or retrieved by the App is stored only in the operator's own private GitHub repository, used solely to improve future content decisions on the same channel.

Third-party services used

This App uses the following third-party services to function:

  • Google YouTube Data API — for uploading and reading video data
  • Groq API — for generating video script text
  • Pexels API — for retrieving stock video footage (no personal data involved)

Revoking access

The operator can revoke this App's access at any time via:
https://myaccount.google.com/permissions
